2. Write detailed answers (0.5 - 1 page of text) to the following two questions: A) In your opinion, why did the Agile manifesto appear? B)What problems did it have to solve and did it succeed?
68
In your opinion, why did the Agile manifesto appear?What problems did it have to solve and did it succeed?
69
70
The Agile manifesto emerged as a response to the problems that arose from the use of old software development methods, such as the Waterfall model. In the 90s, it was clear that traditional methods had significant limitations, especially when it came to large projects. The main problem was that these methods required a lot of time for planning and creating documentation, but did not allow for quick changes to the product if the requirements changed during the work.

In the old methods, product testing and verification often took place only after development was complete, which made it very difficult to make changes at later stages. In particular, when the project was already at the finish line, any adjustments to the requirements became very expensive and time-consuming. Therefore, in 2001, a group of developers created the Agile Manifesto to offer a more flexible and efficient approach to software development. The manifesto emphasizes that the team must be ready to adapt quickly to changes, maintain constant communication, and prioritize performance over documentation.		Agile has significantly changed the approach to software development. Teams became more flexible and could respond to changes more quickly, reducing the cost of adjusting requirements and improving product quality through early testing. However, for some large and complex projects, Agile may be less effective due to the lack of detailed planning and the difficulty in managing large teams. But in general, the Agile methodology has improved the ability of teams to quickly adapt to changes and deliver quality products in a short time.

3. You are the founder of a startup and you are planning to launch a mobile application for sharing cat photos. What methodology will you choose for the development process and why? Justify your answer in text.
79
As a startup founder, I would choose an Agile methodology, namely Scrum or Kanban, to develop a mobile photo sharing app for the following reasons:

1. Flexibility and quick adaptation to changes and edits. In Agile, the team can quickly adapt to changes, so if we get feedback from users or the market changes, we can change the app's features without much delay.
2. Fast launch and testing. Instead of waiting for the entire project to be completed, we can quickly release the first version of the app with basic features (for example, the ability to share cat photos) and then gradually add new features based on user feedback.
3. Quick feedback and team collaboration. In startups, the team is often small, so it's important that all team members interact and work together quickly and effectively. Agile allows for constant communication and quick problem solving.
4. Focus on product quality. In Agile, the product is tested at every stage of development, which allows you to detect errors early and make the application stable.
5. Minimizing risks. Since we work with small parts of the product and get feedback at each stage, it helps to avoid major problems and reduce risks.
